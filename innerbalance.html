Project overview

Making everyday mental-wellness support feel approachable

InnerBalance was created for people navigating stress, loneliness and the stigma surrounding mental-health conversations. The concept brings mood tracking, private reflection, guided relaxation and access to support into one calm mobile experience.

The problem

The Challenge

How might a mobile product support emotional self-care without feeling clinical, overwhelming or judgemental?

Young adults

Users struggle to manage recurring stress privately.

Demanding work and social stigma can make it difficult to acknowledge emotions or seek help.

Students

Academic pressure can grow without accessible support.

Students need simple ways to reflect, build emotional awareness and find appropriate resources.

Wellness resources

Support often feels fragmented or difficult to approach.

A single, culturally sensitive experience can connect private reflection, calming activities and support.

User research

Research shaped the experience before pixels did

The team combined early discovery activities with prototype evaluation. No unsupported percentages are shown; the findings below reflect recurring themes recorded in the project documents.

Research methods

  • Collaborative workshopsDefined the mission, feature set and key journeys.
  • QuestionnaireExplored expectations and prior experience with wellness apps.
  • Task-based usability testingObserved mood logging, journaling and support flows.
  • Think-aloud and interviewsCaptured confusion, emotional comfort and perceived usefulness.
Insight 01

Keep emotional input simple

Emoji-based mood selection made reflection easier to begin without requiring users to articulate complex feelings immediately.

Insight 02

Create clear confirmation

Users needed visible feedback after saving moods, journal entries and other important actions.

Insight 03

Balance calm with usefulness

Early screens felt too empty, leading to richer content, quick actions, daily tips and guided activities.

Insight 04

Standardise the interface

Consistent labels, components, contrast and navigation made the product feel more trustworthy and easier to learn.

Testing & improvements

Feedback became visible product improvements

Testing focused on meaningful tasks such as logging a mood, saving a journal entry and finding community support.

  1. Round 1
    Early prototype

    The interface felt too empty and visually limited.

    Change: Added richer wellness content, quick actions, daily prompts and a warmer purple visual direction.

  2. Round 2
    Mid-fidelity refinement

    Labels, contrast and button placement needed greater clarity.

    Change: Improved typography, accessibility contrast, visual hierarchy and navigation consistency.

  3. Final
    High-fidelity evaluation

    Users needed confidence that important actions were complete.

    Change: Added confirmation feedback, refined microinteractions and standardised components across screens.

Reflection

What the project strengthened

What worked well

A shared focus on user needs helped the team move from a broad mental-wellness challenge to a coherent product direction. Frequent feedback made design decisions easier to explain and defend.

Challenges

Maintaining visual consistency across contributors and balancing useful content with a calm interface required deliberate communication, shared components and prioritisation.

What I learned

I strengthened my ability to connect research with interface decisions, communicate within a design team and treat usability feedback as an input for continuous iteration rather than a final check.
